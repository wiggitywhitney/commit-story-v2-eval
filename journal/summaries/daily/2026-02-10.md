# Daily Summary — 2026-02-10

## Narrative

The developer spent the day building out testing infrastructure for Claude Code interactions. They started with a research phase, analyzing four different implementations of testing and development guardrails across various repositories. This research informed a layered approach: a global safety net with universal rules, a shared config repo with reusable testing tools, and per-project applications of the framework. They documented this strategy in a detailed research document and created PRD #25 to guide the implementation.

With the research complete, they moved into implementation. They configured a global safety net by defining permission rules in `~/.claude/settings.json`, establishing 27 deny rules to block access to sensitive files and destructive commands, 33 allow rules for standard development operations, and 4 "ask" rules for potentially risky actions like pushing code or publishing packages. They considered more complex template systems but decided against them, embracing a simpler approach with sensible defaults that could be overridden at the project level if needed.

Finally, they validated the configuration through live testing. The validation confirmed that most rules worked as intended, though they discovered three non-functional deny patterns related to shell operators (`curl|bash`, `>/dev/*`, and `wget|sh`). They noted these gaps pose low practical risk due to Claude's built-in safety mechanisms and the blanket `sudo` denial. They documented both the working rules and the discovered limitations, marking milestone 2 as complete.

## Key Decisions

- **Layered Testing Infrastructure**: Implement testing across three layers — global safety net, shared config repo, and per-project application — to create portable, adaptable testing strategies that work across different project types.

- **Global Safety Net Configuration**: Define specific deny, allow, and ask rules in `~/.claude/settings.json` rather than creating complex template systems, following a YAGNI approach with sensible defaults that can be overridden at the project level.

- **Document Security Gaps**: Accept three non-functional deny patterns in the permission glob matching system as low-risk given Claude's built-in safety mechanisms and the blanket `sudo` denial, rather than pursuing workarounds.
